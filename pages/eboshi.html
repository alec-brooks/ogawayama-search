Eboshi Iwa - Eboshi Rock

This is the name of the big group of walls and towers high on the hillside above and right of Mara Iwa. While the scale is impressive, the quality and condition of the rock can be disappointing. The left ridge of the area is climbed by Saryousen, by far the longest route at Ogawayama. Further right and starting higher up is the steep wall of Eboshi Main. Right again and lower down are the slabs of Ura Eboshi. Musasabi Route climbs the big broken face up and right again. Eboshi, incidentally, is the name of a traditional type of hat, usually black and knob-shaped.

Cross Nishi Matasawa as for Mara Iwa, but take the first fork right in the path, about 70m up from the stream. This is an old forestry track. About 50m along this, a cairn on the left marks the path for Saryousen. A further 50m along another cairn marks the path left for Eboshi Main and Ura Eboshi. After 200m climbing pleasantly through the woods, another cairn marks the left fork to Eboshi Main. This path is quite well marked with red tape, which is a bit of a waste because nobody goes there.
If you stay on the old forestry track, you will eventually hit the main stream again and the Kimpu-san Highway at Phoenix Rock.

At some stage, I will add a map and/or topo, but until then, these descriptions will have to do.

Saryousen

Altogether, this should take about 25 minutes from the campsite. From the old forestry track, take the first path leading left into the woods. The turning is prominently marked with a cairn and smaller cairns lead you quickly through the woods to the base of a scree slope. Follow the marked path up this till, about level with the top of Mara Iwa on your left, the path crosses the (usually) dry stream on the right and heads back into woods around the base of the ridge. The descent route will not bring you back this way, so only carry what you need on the rock after you leave the old forestry track.

Saryousen - Left Ridge 5.8*** 400m
An extremely long, ultimately satisfying outing, featuring walks, abseils, loose rock, superlative views and a lot of easy climbing. Take a full rack and a lot of slings.
I'll write this up if and when I do it! In the meantime, there is a topo in the latest edition of the Kitayama guide.

Eboshi Main

This is the clutch of steep faces well right of Saryousen (which climbs across the top of these routes). Unfortunately, the rock is not very good. The main section is distinguishable as a steep slender face with a jagged overlap at two-thirds height.

From the old forestry track, continue past the cairn that marks the path for Saryousen for about 50m to another cairn. Follow the path left into the woods for about 200m to another prominent cairn. Take the left fork. This steeper path is quite well marked with red tape (in 2012) and leads in about 150m to a narrow collapsing bay below a steep bolted wall.

Hyoutantsugi - ??? 5.11a* 25m
A line based on a couple of grooves right of the bounding corner of the face.
Start on a crumbling ledge and climb easily to the first groove. Follow new and old bolts into the second groove to finish at a lower-off well below the big overlap.

Eboshi de Hiboshi - Sunbaked on Eboshi 5.11b** 25m
The steep face between the grooves of Hyoutantsugi and the crack of Nankayoukai. Highly rated by the first ascensionist.
Climb a wide crumbling crack behind a big tree to get established on the rib. Mantel right then follow the bolts left then right to a lower-off.

Nankayoukai - What Do You Want? 5.11a* 50m
The line based on the intermittent wide but shallow crack in the centre of the face. Unfortunately crumbly in places. Take a bunch of Friends.
Pitch 1. 5.10d Follow the crack to an anchor below the big overlap.
Pitch 2. 5.11a Continue up the crack, pull through the overlap, then follow the continuation crack to an anchor.
To descend, ab the route.

West Kante - West Arete 5.11a* 50m
The right arete of the face.
Pitch 1. 5.10d Climb out right to the arete and follow it steeply to an anchor.
Pitch 2. 5.11a Continue.
To descend, ab the route.

Last Resort 5.10d* 70m
The groove/crack overlooked by West Kante. Crumbly in places and very dirty at the start. Take a full rack.
Pitch 1. 5.10a (30m) From below and right of the line, climb up and traverse left to the beginning of the crack (very dirty) then climb the crack to a good tree.
Pitch 2. 5.10d (40m) Climb the continuation crack to the top. Sustained and bold in parts.
To descend, ab the route.

Mori no Tenshi - Forest Angel 5.12a** 70m
The long bolted line a few metres right of Last Resort. The first pitch is highly rated.
Pitch 1. 5.10c Follow the bolts through no distinct line to belay on a sloping ledge.
Pitch 2. 5.11a Climb the continuation bolt ladder to another anchor.
Pitch 3. 5.12a Climb the continuation bolt ladder to another anchor.
To descend, ab the route.

Ura Eboshi

This is the south side of the Eboshi rocks, not really distinguishable from the campsite. The routes were heavily cleaned for the first ascents, but the base of the rocks is densely wooded and the moss is creeping back.

Approach as for Eboshi Main, but where the path splits, take the right fork. This path is also quite well marked and leads in about 150m to a broad bay with a seeping rock fang below prominent cracks (the start of Rocketman). It should take about 1 hour from the campsite. For Sky Pilot, head up to the left of the bay then cut back in above it. The route effectively starts above Bunsouou.

Sky Pilot 5.12a*** 22m
The steep face tucked away high on the left.
Climb an arete then the face above via 10 bolts to a lower-off.

Back down in the bay:

Bunsouou - Within my Means 5.10b* 18m
The slim slabby rib on the left of the bay. Take Friends #2 and #4 for the start.
Climb a wide crumbling crack behind a big tree to get established on the rib at an obvious ear of rock. Mantel right (according to the topo) or left (according to me) then follow the bolts to a lower-off. At the given grade, this appears to involve a lot of dipping in to the partially cleaned wide crack on the left.

Jiku - Axis 5.13a* 17m
The thin crack in the leaning headwall right of Bunsouou. Take a rack with a lot of smalls.
Climb easily to the base of the crack, clip a bolt then follow the crack to another bolt at its end and pull out left to join Bunsouou just below its lower-off.

Eboshi Layback 5.8** 18m
The wide corner crack in the sidewall. Take a set of Friends, with lots of big ones.
Pull up to the crack as for Jiku, then layback all the way to a lower-off on a small ledge. Intimidating, but good.

Suzushii Kao - Cool Face 5.11c* 15m
Climbs the slightly overhanging sidewall direct to the lower-off of Eboshi Layback.
Start well back in the gloom and follow the bolts up the leaning wall past a crumbly section to the lower-off of Eboshi Layback.

Touhan no Subete III - All About Climbing III 5.10c*** 25m
Climbs the slab opposite Eboshi Layback via a delightful shallow flake. Take a rack with a lot of small cams.
Start below a bulging wide cleft with a wedged block. Pull up to a bolt on its left, place some big cams in the cleft then follow the cleft over and left to the base of the flake. Climb this to a bolt at its end, then smear up the slick waterworn slab to the headwall. Swing out left and pass 2 bolts to a good tree and lower-off.

Rocketman 5.10c* 26m
A highly rated (by the first ascensionist, at least) multi-pitch route which is rarely repeated due to its repulsive start.
Start below the seeping fang of rock a few metres right of the bulging cleft of Touhan no Subete III. Climb to the fang then step left into the central of the three cracks. Follow this to a good tree on a terrace.
Not sure where the continuation pitches go.

The next rock to the right has been christened Zen'ei Dome (Vanguard Dome). The starts of all the routes are quickly returning to their natural mossy state.

Mado kara Haidase - Crawling out the Window 5.11c* 26m
Another attractive, highly rated (by the first ascensionist) route which will need a good clean if you plan to do it.
Climb the dirty slab to a crumbly bulge. Pull over and traverse right, then go up to a dike and back left. Finish direct up the slab.

Miss Natural 5.10b*** 35m
The big open groove in the top slab is superb. Unfortunately, getting there is not. Take a rack and save something big for the trough at the top.
Start directly below the groove and climb a short steep face to a short slab with a descent anchor. Step right, move up to a ledge, then step back left to a bolt. Pull steeply up to the base of the groove and follow it past all manner of goodies till it curves left and peters out at a slab. Step up into a trough and mantel right out of this to gain a lower-off. I got down on the stretch on a 60m rope, but be prepared to use the anchor at 10m.

Crystal Ship 5.11b** 26m
An open line up the steep right edge of the face.
Start just left of the right edge of the lower face. Scramble up a gully then pull left as quickly as possible and follow the bolts steeply to a lower-off.

Musasabi Wall

This is an extension of the Eboshi Iwa massif, distinguishable by the ochre wall with wide crack that provides the highlight of Musasabi Route. Musasabi, by the way, is a type of flying squirrel.

Approach as for Ura Eboshi, but skirt these rocks on the right, and continue about 100m up and right to the base of a low-angled crumbly slab. This is the original first pitch, but it is better to continue up and right then cut back in to the top of this slab. At this level, there is a ring bolt and peg belay on the left below a thin crack leading to a bottomless groove. The line is also marked by a hanger bolt and several pegs. Your landmark is the ochre wall with wide crack way up and up.

Musasabi Route 5.10c*** 150m
A rarely climbed route that is well worth the effort for the fourth pitch and the views from the top. Take a rack. Wires are useful to back up the pegs on the first pitch and a couple of Friends #3 and #4 will make you happier on Itachi Crack.
Pitch 1. 5.10b Climb over an overlap to reach the thin crack, then make a tricky entry into the groove. Follow this to its end then step left to twin bolts.
Pitch 2. 5.8 Go easily up and left to a loose, dirty corner and climb this to exit onto a mini-forest and belay at the top of a pinnacle.
Pitch 3. 5.10c Climb the left edge of the slab (sling in the tree?) then head right to a distant bolt. Step up to a pancake flake (cams) then palm the edge of the slab to a small ledge and bolt. Make a long reach and technical move to gain and climb a short crack then follow the easier slab to belay on a stumpy tree and bomber cams below an impressive crack. (Japanese guides describe this as two 5.9 pitches by continuing diagonally right from the first bolt to belay on a good ledge with a monster pine then climbing back left to the second bolt. But there's no way that move past the short crack is 5.9. I have spoken to some climbers who said they dodged the hard move by staying left.)
Pitch 4. 5.10a Climb the steep wide crack directly above the belay - Itachi Crack (Weasel Crack). Excellent gear and jams, but high in the grade nonetheless! The original route follows a line up dirty cracks to the left at 5.8.
Pitch 5.     Scramble back to a tree with ab point, then continue a long way up left to the summit.
To descend, first return to the ab point at the top of Pitch 4. From this, ab 20m (down the gully, NOT down the face you just climbed up) to another tree with ab point, then ab 20m over wedged blocks to another tree with ab point, then ab 35m to the ground a short way up the slope from Pitch 1. There is another tree at 20m to split the last ab, but I wouldn't recommend it.
